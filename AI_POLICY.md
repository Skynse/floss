AI is not welcome here, at least not anymore.

 Like everything else, if you use AI for 70% of the process, you understand less and less of the codebase. Fortunately, in the case of Floss, it's not hard to add features, and I'd like to keep it that way. AI was used early on in Floss to scaffold, but it was not one-shotted and took a few months to get to the current state.

I've realized that the more you use AI for things, the more reliant you are on it to get things accomplished. What happens if you can't afford hardware to run the models, or don't want to shell out $10 every time to pay for tokens? The verdict is that, it's better to just code manually, at least most of the time. AI has its place sometimes, but what's the rush?

If you do use AI to make PRs on this project, ensure that it is completely assistance, not just slop. For example, deleting stubs, adding docstrings, code refactors can be done with AI if manually reviewed. New features must be planned slowly and carefully.
